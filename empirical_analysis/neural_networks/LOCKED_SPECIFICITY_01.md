# SFH-SGP_SPECIFICITY_01 (CORRECTED LOCK)

## Systems Tested

1. Logistic Map (chaotic, λ transition)
2. Kuramoto Model (continuous synchronization)
3. Ornstein-Uhlenbeck Process (continuous stochastic)

## Results

| System | D(k) Collapse |
|--------|-------------|
| Logistic Map | YES (in periodic windows, λ < 0) |
| Kuramoto | NO |
| Ornstein-Uhlenbeck | NO |

---

## Interpretation (STRICT)

D(k) collapse occurs in the logistic map in regimes where λ < 0 
(periodic dynamics), and is absent in tested continuous-transition systems 
(Kuramoto, OU) under identical embedding and analysis conditions.

Important:

This result does NOT establish that D(k) collapse is a universal detector 
of chaos or discontinuous transitions.

---

## Working Hypothesis

D(k) collapse reflects low-dimensional or highly constrained trajectory 
structure (e.g., periodic orbits), rather than chaos itself.
