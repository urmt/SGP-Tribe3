# SFH-SGP_OPERATOR_EQUIVALENCE_01 (LOCKED)

## Research Question
Do operators partition into equivalence classes under Φ(S, O)?

## Test Design
- 3 Systems: chaotic (logistic), periodic (sine), stochastic (Gaussian)
- 19 Operators: linear, nonlinear, normalization, mixed family
- Metric: Euclidean distance in (α, DET) space

## Results

| System | Unique Φ / Total | Equivalence Pairs | Best k |
|--------|-----------------|-------------------|--------|
| Chaotic | 16/19 (84%) | 15.2% | 2 |
| Periodic | 16/19 (84%) | 11.1% | 2 |
| Stochastic | 16/19 (84%) | 17.5% | 3 |

## Key Finding

**No strong equivalence collapse** - 84% of operators produce unique Φ values.

- Mixed family operators (λx + (1-λ)sin(x)) show continuous variation
- Some clustering exists (k=2-3), but not equivalence classes

## Interpretation

ℴ does NOT partition into discrete equivalence classes:
- Most operators map to distinct Φ values
- Continuous operator families (mixed) produce continuous Φ variation
- No "collapse" of distinct operators to same observable

## Conclusion

Operator space ℴ under Φ is NOT discrete/stratified - it is CONTINUOUS in structure.

This is the opposite of what was hypothesized - instead of equivalence classes, we find a near-continuous mapping from operator space to observable space.
