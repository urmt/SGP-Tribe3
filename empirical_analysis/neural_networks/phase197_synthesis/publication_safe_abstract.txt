PUBLICATION-SAFE ABSTRACT
==========================

We tested whether EEG synchrony structure can be reduced to lower-order feature 
combinations using cross-channel alignment metrics. Across 30+ tested preservation 
models (single-feature, pairwise, triple, and quadruple combinations), no reduced-
order model preserved eigenvalue/efficiency metrics within the 15% survival 
threshold. The organizational structure requires simultaneous presence of five 
factors: zero-lag synchrony, temporal propagation ordering, phase-locking value, 
coalition persistence, and burst coincidence timing. This five-factor dependency 
generalizes across 8 of 10 tested observables but is not universal. The findings 
support a multi-factor alignment requirement for synchrony structure preservation.
