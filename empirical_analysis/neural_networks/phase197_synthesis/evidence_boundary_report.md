# EVIDENCE BOUNDARY REPORT - PHASE 197

## DEFINED EVIDENCE BOUNDARY

### WHAT THE EVIDENCE SUPPORTS

1. **Multi-factor synchrony structure**: The EEG eigenvalue/efficiency metrics (O1-O8) require simultaneous presence of five specific inter-channel alignment mechanisms. The evidence strongly supports this claim.

2. **Failed low-order reduction**: All tested reduced-order preservation models (single-feature: 5, pairwise: 10, triple: 10, quadruple: 5 = 30 total) failed to preserve structure under 15% survival threshold. The evidence strongly supports that no subset of features is sufficient.

3. **Non-causal factors eliminated**: Kurtosis, common-mode artifact, and internal burst timing have been empirically falsified as causal explanations. The evidence supports these being non-causal.

4. **Observable-specific boundary**: The irreducibility generalizes to 8 of 10 tested metrics. Two metrics (propagation asymmetry, graph entropy) are preserved under reduced-order models. The evidence supports this as a genuine structural difference, not metric artifact.

### WHAT THE EVIDENCE DOES NOT SUPPORT

1. **Universal irreducibility**: The evidence does NOT support claiming irreducibility across ALL possible observables - 2 of 10 metrics showed preservation.

2. **Consciousness/ cognition claims**: No evidence supports any psychological or cognitive interpretation. All claims are constrained to "synchrony structure" or "eigenvalue organization".

3. **Biological uniqueness**: No comparison was made to other biological or synthetic systems. The evidence cannot support uniqueness claims.

4. **Mechanism completion**: The five factors are identified but their dynamic interaction mechanism is unknown. The evidence supports WHAT is required, not HOW it works.

### UNKNOWN/UNRESOLVED

1. **Generalizability**: Whether this dependency holds for other EEG datasets (different populations, recordings, preprocessing)

2. **Temporal dynamics**: How the five factors interact in real-time; whether their relative importance shifts

3. **Threshold stability**: Whether the 15% survival threshold is robust or arbitrary

4. **Causal mechanism**: What dynamical system properties create this requirement

## PUBLICATION-SAFE BOUNDARY

The evidence is constrained to:
- CHB-MIT dataset (4 subjects from Phase 112)
- FFT-based phase computation
- 10% burst threshold
- Weighted adjacency matrices (no thresholding)
- 15% survival criterion
- 10 specific metrics (O1-O10)

Claims cannot generalize beyond this framework without additional empirical validation.
