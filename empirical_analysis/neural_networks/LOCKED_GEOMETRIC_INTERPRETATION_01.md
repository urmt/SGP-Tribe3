# SFH-SGP_GEOMETRIC_INTERPRETATION_01

## Observation

In the logistic map, D(k) becomes constant across k (collapse) in periodic regimes.

## Hypothesis

D(k) collapse corresponds to regimes where the system trajectory occupies 
a low-support subset of state space (e.g., periodic orbits or highly 
constrained manifolds).

## Supporting Evidence

- Logistic periodic windows → collapse observed
- Chaotic regime (Hénon) → NO collapse
- Kuramoto / OU → no collapse

## Implication

D(k) is sensitive to attractor geometry, not just transition type or complexity.

## Status

**WORKING HYPOTHESIS**
Requires cross-system validation

- The collapse appears to detect periodic/low-dimensional attractors
- Does NOT detect general chaotic dynamics (falsified with Hénon)
