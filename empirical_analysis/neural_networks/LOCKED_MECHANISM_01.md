# SFH-SGP_MECHANISM_01 (LOCKED)

## Empirical Finding

D(k) collapse occurs in the logistic map during periodic regimes (λ < 0),
but is absent in:

- Hénon map (chaotic, λ > 0)
- Kuramoto model (continuous synchronization)
- Ornstein-Uhlenbeck process (stochastic)

---

## Conclusion

D(k) collapse is not a general detector of chaos.

---

## Working Mechanism

D(k) collapse occurs when system trajectories occupy a highly constrained,
low-support subset of state space (e.g., periodic orbits or discrete cycles),
leading to scale-invariant neighbor structure.

---

## Implication

D(k) is sensitive to attractor geometry (support structure),
not directly to chaos, complexity, or transition type.
