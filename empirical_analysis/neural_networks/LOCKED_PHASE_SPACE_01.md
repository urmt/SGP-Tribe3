# SFH-SGP_PHASE_SPACE_MAPPING_01 (LOCKED)

## Research Question
Does (α, DET) space reveal geometric structure of dynamical transitions?

## Systems Swept
1. Logistic map: r ∈ [3.4, 4.0]
2. Kuramoto model: K ∈ [0, 3]
3. Ornstein-Uhlenbeck: θ ∈ [0.1, 2.0]

## Results

| System | α range | DET range | Behavior |
|--------|---------|----------|----------|
| Logistic | 0.39 → 0.94 | 0.63 → 1.0 | **Transition** |
| Kuramoto | 0.83 → 1.00 | 0.26 → 0.90 | Smooth |
| OU | 0.99 → 1.00 | 0.11 | **Constant** |

---

## Geometric Structure

**Logistic**: Sharp transition at r ≈ 3.57 (chaos onset)
- α jumps from ~0.4 to ~0.9
- DET shows discontinuity

**Kuramoto**: Smooth continuous curve
- Both α and DET vary continuously with K
- No sharp boundary

**OU**: Constant trajectory
- α ≈ 1.0 (stochastic baseline)
- DET ≈ 0.11 (noise-dominated)

---

## Interpretation

(α, DET) space reveals:
- **Discrete vs continuous** dynamical transitions
- **Stochastic baselines** as distinct regions

This is a valid dynamical phase diagram.
