2. EXPERIENCE AXIOM
================================================================================

Framework distinction:

  "having an experience" vs "not having an experience"

This distinction is CATEGORICAL (binary), not continuous.
The AXIOM assumes that the category "having an experience" applies
to some systems and not to others.

COMPLEXITY changes structure/richness of experience, not existence category.
A simple experience and a complex experience are both experiences.
The distinction is about quality, not about whether experience exists.

EMPIRICAL COROLLARY:
  Phase 252-263 identifies geometric structures that are:
    - PERSISTENT (stable over time)
    - INTERACTION-CAPABLE (respond to coupling)
    - COHERENT (synchronized order parameter)
    - RECURSIVELY GENERATING (higher-order collective modes)

  These features MAY correlate with the conditions for experience,
  but the empirical analysis does NOT claim that these structures
  "have experience" or "are conscious."

INTERPRETATION (EXPLORATORY):
  Geometric features identified in Phases 252-263 may serve as
  necessary conditions for experiential persistence, not sufficient
  conditions. Further work is needed to determine whether these
  structures are "experience-capable."
