REPLICATION FAILURE CONDITIONS — PHASE 263
============================================

Purpose: Define exactly what constitutes a failed replication,
         what triggers tier downgrades, and what forces audit suspension.

All conditions are enforced at the dataset level.

================================================================================
1. CONDITIONS THAT INVALIDATE A REPLICATION
================================================================================

A replication is INVALIDATED (not just failed) if any of the
following occur. Invalidated datasets are archived but not used
in the manuscript.

INV1: Critical physics violation
  - R(K) exceeds 1.0 anywhere (unphysical)
  - R(K) is negative anywhere (unphysical)
  - K takes negative values
  - N takes non-integer or non-positive values
  - Timestep dt <= 0

INV2: Preprocessing contamination
  - Kuramoto fitting applied before order parameter computation
  - Frequency data filtered or smoothed before simulation
  - Oscillators removed based on synchronization level
  - R normalized to target range [0,1] before analysis

INV3: Missing audit chain
  - Figures not SHA256-hashed
  - Scripts not archived
  - No metadata record
  - Audit trace incomplete

INV4: Metadata fraud
  - Claimed N, K, STEPS values that do not match outputs
  - Fabricated trial data
  - Modified figures after computation

================================================================================
2. CONDITIONS THAT DOWNGRADE TIER 1 CLAIMS
================================================================================

A Tier 1 claim (C1-C6) is DOWNGRADED to Tier 2 or removed
if a replication dataset produces the following outcomes:

TIER DOWNGRADE TRIGGERS:

C1 (Transition exists):
  - FAIL on F1 criterion in the replication dataset
  - Action: C1 becomes Tier 2; "transition exists" language only
    if F1 shows increase but does not reach R > 0.7

C2 (No hysteresis):
  - FAIL on F3 criterion (loop_area > 0.25)
  - Action: C2 is REMOVED; hysteresis must be investigated in future work

C3 (Finite-size sharpening):
  - FAIL on F2 AND replication shows FLATTER transition at larger N
  - Action: C3 is REMOVED; "sharpening" claim not supported by replication

C4 (Critical slowing):
  - FAIL on F6 AND tau flat or decreasing with K
  - Action: C4 is REMOVED; critical slowing not confirmed

C5 (Topology robustness):
  - FAIL on F7 AND alternative topology shows NO transition
  - Action: C5 is REMOVED; topology limitation must be stated

C6 (Binder structure):
  - FAIL on F4 AND U4 = 0.67 (Gaussian) across entire K range
  - Action: C6 is REMOVED; Binder structure not observed

TIER DOWNGRADE PROCESS:
  - Downgrade requires FAIL on the corresponding criterion
  - INCONCLUSIVE on a criterion does NOT trigger downgrade
  - Downgrade is dataset-specific (Phase 252-256 claims remain valid
    for the original dataset; replication dataset gets its own verdict)
  - Each dataset is assessed independently

================================================================================
3. CONDITIONS THAT DOWNGRADE TIER 2 CLAIMS
================================================================================

A Tier 2 claim (C7-C10) is DOWNGRADED or REMOVED if:

C7 (gamma ~ 1.1):
  - FAIL on F5 criterion (chi_peak does not increase with N)
  - OR gamma computed is < 0.3 or > 3.0
  - Action: C7 removed; gamma is dataset-specific, not universal

C8 (data collapse):
  - Kc or nu cannot be estimated from replication data
  - Action: C8 remains Tier 2; "approximate" language only

C9 (Kc ~ 1.4):
  - K estimates shift by more than 1.0 across N in replication
  - Action: C9 remains Tier 2 but confidence reduced
  - Three-estimator convergence failed in replication (like Phase 256)

C10 (gamma varies by frequency):
  - Both frequency distributions produce same gamma (diff < 0.3)
  - Action: C10 removed; gamma does not vary as observed

================================================================================
4. CONDITIONS THAT FORCE AUDIT SUSPENSION
================================================================================

Audit is SUSPENDED if any of the following occur.
Suspended audits are frozen and reviewed by the audit council
before resuming.

SUSP1: Systematic bias detected
  - RNG seed produces systematically better results than seed 42
  - Multiple seeds produce inconsistent verdicts (e.g., seed 0: PASS,
    seed 100: FAIL on same criterion)
  - Implies results are seed-dependent artifacts

SUSP2: Parameter drift detected
  - Dataset uses different STEPS than admitted dataset
  - Metadata shows K range shifted after initial run
  - Implies post-hoc parameter tuning

SUSP3: Cross-dataset conflict
  - Two or more datasets produce contradictory verdicts on F1
  - One dataset: strong transition; another: no transition
  - Both datasets pass admission rules
  - Requires audit council review

SUSP4: Failure cascade
  - More than 2 FAIL verdicts across all structures
  - Implies fundamental difference between datasets
  - Requires investigation before proceeding

SUSP5: Figure tampering
  - SHA256 hash mismatch on any figure
  - Figure modified after initial computation
  - Triggers full audit review

AUDIT SUSPENSION PROCESS:
  1. Identify triggering condition
  2. Freeze all manuscript writing pending review
  3. Document triggering condition in suspension_report.txt
  4. Form audit council (at minimum: lead analyst + 1 independent reviewer)
  5. Council reviews evidence and determines:
     - RESUME: condition was false positive or fixable
     - CORRECT: condition is real but correctable
     - TERMINATE: condition is fundamental and unrecoverable
  6. Council decision is documented and archived
  7. Manuscript proceeds only after RESUME or CORRECT decision

================================================================================
5. REPLICATION VERDICT FAILURE MAP
================================================================================

Per-structure failure conditions:

F1 (Transition):
  - FAIL if: R_max - R_min < 0.1 across K range
  - FAIL if: R does not increase monotonically
  - FAIL if: R_max < 0.5 at maximum K

F2 (Sharpening):
  - FAIL if: dR/dK is flat or decreasing with N
  - FAIL if: largest N shows flatter transition than smallest N

F3 (Hysteresis):
  - FAIL if: loop_area > 0.25
  - FAIL if: gap > 0.25 at any K
  - FAIL if: backward sweep consistently above forward sweep

F4 (Binder):
  - FAIL if: U4 ≈ 0.67 everywhere (no structure)
  - FAIL if: U4 > 0.67 anywhere (supergaussian)
  - FAIL if: U4 monotonically decreases with K (no recovery)

F5 (Chi scaling):
  - FAIL if: chi_peak does not increase with N
  - FAIL if: gamma < 0.3 or gamma > 3.0
  - FAIL if: chi(K) has no peak

F6 (Critical slowing):
  - FAIL if: tau flat or decreasing with K
  - FAIL if: peak_ratio < 1.5
  - FAIL if: tau saturates and no peak identifiable

F7 (Topology):
  - FAIL if: alternative topology shows no transition
  - FAIL if: R(K) shape fundamentally different between topologies

F8 (Frequency):
  - FAIL if: one frequency distribution shows no transition
  - FAIL if: gamma < 0.3 or gamma > 3.0 for any distribution

================================================================================
6. FAILURE REPORTING REQUIREMENTS
================================================================================

Every FAIL verdict requires a failure_report.txt in the dataset
directory containing:

1. Criterion that failed
2. Measured values that triggered FAIL
3. Threshold values that defined FAIL
4. Figures showing the failure
5. Hypothesis for why failure occurred
6. Whether failure is likely due to:
   a. Genuine physical difference (new physics)
   b. Methodological difference (parameter issue)
   c. Implementation error (bug)
   d. Audit error (false positive)
7. Recommended action (continue, modify, terminate)

FAILURE REPORTS ARE MANDATORY.
A dataset with FAIL verdicts but no failure_report.txt is
flagged as SUSPENDED.

================================================================================
7. FAILURE ESCALATION THRESHOLDS
================================================================================

2 FAIL verdicts:
  - Dataset receives INCONCLUSIVE overall verdict
  - Phase 252-256 claims remain unchanged
  - Future work triggered for the specific failing structures

4 FAIL verdicts:
  - Dataset receives FAIL overall verdict
  - Phase 252-256 claims are reviewed for generalization
  - Cross-dataset conflict may trigger SUSP3

6+ FAIL verdicts:
  - SUSP4 triggered: audit suspension
  - Full audit council review required
