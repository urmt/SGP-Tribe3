INTERPRETATION:

1. Amplitude (A) shows a SIGNIFICANT difference between task and rest conditions (p < 0.0001). The positive effect size (Cohen's d = 2.98) indicates that amplitude is substantially higher during task than during rest. This is consistent with the hypothesis that cognitive engagement increases representational capacity.

2. Midpoint (k0) shows a marginally significant increase during task (p = 0.053). This suggests that the integration scale shifts toward larger spatial scales during active processing.

3. Steepness (beta) shows a marginally significant decrease during task (p = 0.057). This suggests a more gradual accumulation of dimensionality during task, possibly reflecting distributed processing.

CAVEATS:
- The rest condition amplitude values are at a boundary (-1000), which affects variance estimates
- The sample size is small (N = 10), limiting statistical power
- Multiple parameters tested without correction for multiple comparisons
- One subject (sub-07) shows outlier behavior in steepness

ROBUSTNESS:

- Bootstrap CI excludes zero for all parameters

CONCLUSION:
The empirical data support significant task-related differences in amplitude, with marginal differences in k0 and beta. Amplitude is the most robustly affected parameter, showing a large and reliable increase during task engagement compared to rest.
